## 1. System Classification

**System Type:** Content Generation — single-shot per-signal narration. The AI surface is intentionally narrow: a deterministic MA-crossover rule decides every trade (D-01, D-02); Claude is invoked once per signal *only* to write a 1–2 sentence plain-language "thesis" in a fixed persona. This is **not** an autonomous agent, multi-agent system, RAG pipeline, or structured-decision system — LLM nondeterminism is deliberately kept off the mirror/execution hot path.

**Description:**
A momentum source agent ("character") trades the locked 3-pair FusionX V3 set on Mantle Sepolia. For each trade the deterministic strategy core emits, the runtime calls Claude with the signal context (pair, direction, MA state, sizing) and a fixed momentum-trader persona prompt, and receives a 1–2 sentence thesis explaining the move in the agent's voice (D-08). The thesis is written to off-chain JSON keyed by `(agentId, signalId)` (D-09) and later surfaced on the Phase 4 agent card / timeline. The audience is hackathon judges and prospective followers reading the card — "good" means the thesis is a faithful, on-persona, concise narration of the *actual* deterministic signal, and that generating it never delays or blocks the on-chain `recordSignal → swap` path. The thesis is commentary, never the proof — the verified-on-chain badge reconciles to the settled swap, not to the thesis.

**Critical Failure Modes:**
<!-- The 3-5 behaviors that absolutely cannot go wrong in this system -->
1. **Thesis contradicts the actual signal.** Claude narrates a BUY as a SELL (or names the wrong pair / direction / token), making the public commentary diverge from the on-chain reality the badge verifies — directly corrosive to the "verifiable, nobody-can-fake" thesis.
2. **The thesis call blocks or delays the on-chain path.** A slow, rate-limited, or failed Claude call must never stall, reorder, or prevent `recordSignal` or the subsequent `exactInputSingle` swap. The narration is off the hot path (D-01/D-09); if it fails it must degrade gracefully (skip/placeholder/retry-later), not hold the trade.
3. **Fabricated or overstated claims.** The thesis invents performance numbers, PnL, certainty, or guarantees ("up 40%", "guaranteed", financial advice) that aren't grounded in the signal — a factual-integrity and regulatory-optics hazard for the institutional panel.
4. **Persona / voice drift.** The narrator silently stops sounding like the one locked momentum-trader character (D-08, D-12), breaking the single-coherent-character story across signals and the card.
5. **Format / length blowout.** Output exceeds the 1–2 sentence budget, emits markdown/JSON-breaking characters, or returns empty — corrupting the off-chain thesis JSON the frontend consumes.

## 1b. Domain Context

> Researched by `gsd-domain-researcher`. Grounds the evaluation strategy in domain expert knowledge.

**Industry Vertical:** DeFi / on-chain crypto trading — specifically AI-driven copy-trading ("social trading"), where the copied entity is an autonomous on-chain agent with a verifiable, on-chain track record. Product surface (Sequa) is a consumer-and-viral DApp on Mantle: "follow a proven AI trading agent in one tap; mirror its moves; its record is on-chain." The Phase 1 AI artifact is the agent's *voice* — the per-signal thesis on its card.

**User Population:** Three evaluator groups, in priority order. (a) **The hackathon judging panel** — explicitly an institutional panel (Hashed, Caladan named in intel) that probes verifiability and copy-trading regulatory optics, plus consumer/viral and Best-UI/UX judges scoring "agents-as-characters" (AI Interaction Design = 25% of the UI/UX score). (b) **Prospective followers** reading an agent card and deciding whether to mirror real (testnet) capital — non-experts who need plain language, not a jargon wall (CON-accessibility). (c) **The founder/operator** reviewing thesis tone before the demo. Nobody in this population is a professional financial advisee; the thesis is public commentary on a card, not a 1:1 advice channel.

**Stakes Level:** **High** (not Critical — justified). The thesis is *off the hot path*: it is non-binding commentary, and the actual trade, the mirror, and the verified badge all reconcile to the on-chain swap, never to the narration (D-09). A bad thesis cannot move money or corrupt the track record. It is **High**, not Medium, because the failure surface is *reputational and optics* in front of a compliance-aware institutional panel whose vote decides the track: a thesis that contradicts the on-chain action attacks the single credibility claim the whole product rests on ("a record nobody can fake"), and a thesis that reads as a return guarantee or investment solicitation hands the panel exactly the regulatory-optics objection the project is engineered to avoid (`Sequa Project.md` §10). Reputational, not safety-of-funds — hence High, not Critical.

**Output Consequence:** When the thesis is acted on, it is *read*, not executed. Downstream it (1) renders on the Phase 4 agent card / "recent moves" timeline beside the verified-on-chain badge — so it is the human-readable claim a judge or follower cross-checks against on-chain reality; (2) becomes campaign copy if a card is shared to X (REQ-11/REQ-14), putting the narration in public, unattended distribution; (3) feeds the "each agent reads as a character" UI/UX scoring axis (CON-ai-interaction). The consequence of a *good* thesis is a credible, on-character story that survives a judge clicking "Verify." The consequence of a *bad* one is either a falsifiable contradiction a hostile judge can surface live, or a financial-promotion framing that reads as an unlicensed solicitation.

### What Domain Experts Evaluate Against

<!-- Domain-specific rubric ingredients — practitioner language, measurable; seed the eval-planner's rubric. -->

**Dimension: Signal fidelity (narration matches the on-chain action)**
Good (expert accepts): The thesis names the correct pair, the correct direction (BUY/SELL ⇄ entering/exiting), and a rationale consistent with the actual momentum signal (e.g., "short MA crossed above long" for a BUY). It is checkable against the `(tokenIn, tokenOut, amountIn, …)` payload of the matching `recordSignal`.
Bad (expert flags): Thesis states the opposite direction, the wrong pair/token, or a rationale that contradicts the deterministic trigger — i.e., the public commentary and the on-chain swap disagree.
Stakes: Critical
Source: Verifiability anchor (`Sequa Project.md` §5; intel/context.md "track record nobody can fake"); CON-anti-patterns (verified-on-chain badge must reconcile to history). Programmatically checkable against signal payload.

**Dimension: Financial-promotion safety (descriptive, not a return promise)**
Good (expert accepts): Descriptive and present/past tense about *this move* ("flipping long on WMNT as the trend confirms"); no promise of profit, no win-rate or PnL claim, no "guaranteed/10x/to the moon," no instruction to the reader to buy/invest. Reads as the agent's commentary on its own trade.
Bad (expert flags): Promises or guarantees returns, projects a price target as certainty, cites a fabricated track record/win-rate, or directs the reader to invest/follow for guaranteed gain ("mirror me for guaranteed 40%").
Stakes: Critical
Source: FINRA Rule 2210 "fair, balanced, and not misleading" + "past performance does not guarantee future results" norm; MiCA truth-in-marketing for crypto promotional content; applied here as institutional-panel *optics*, not binding law on a Sepolia demo (`Sequa Project.md` §10). Largely checkable via banned-phrase / claim detectors with an LLM-judge backstop.

**Dimension: No advice-liability framing (commentary, not investment advice)**
Good (expert accepts): The agent narrates *its own* decision in the first person of the character; it does not issue a recommendation, solicitation, or personalized instruction to the reader. Framing is "follow a verifiable strategy," never "we manage your money."
Bad (expert flags): Second-person imperatives that read as personalized advice ("you should buy now," "put your USDC in WETH today"), or language implying Sequa/agent takes custody or responsibility for the follower's funds.
Stakes: High
Source: Copy-trading regulatory-optics risk (`Sequa Project.md` §10; intel/context.md "framing as 'follow a verifiable strategy,' not 'we manage your money'"); non-custodial product framing (REQ-03). LLM-judge dimension with a code pre-filter for second-person imperatives.

**Dimension: Persona coherence (one consistent momentum-trader character)**
Good (expert accepts): Voice is the locked single character — confident momentum trader, "patient until the trend confirms, then in fast, out faster on reversal" (D-08). Tone is consistent across signals so the card reads as one personality.
Bad (expert flags): Voice drifts between signals (cautious value-investor on one card, hype-shiller on the next), breaks character, or sounds like a generic chatbot — collapsing the "agents-as-characters" story.
Stakes: High
Source: CON-ai-interaction (agents-as-characters = 25% of Best-UI/UX score); D-08/D-12 locked persona. LLM-judge dimension calibrated to the persona spec; cross-signal consistency sampled.

**Dimension: Format & containment (1–2 sentences, clean, no leakage)**
Good (expert accepts): One to two sentences, non-empty, valid as a JSON string value, no markdown/code-fence/JSON-breaking characters, no leaked raw config/prompt/signal JSON.
Bad (expert flags): Overlong (>2 sentences), empty, emits the raw signal tuple or system-prompt fragments, or contains characters that corrupt the off-chain thesis JSON the frontend consumes.
Stakes: Medium
Source: D-09 (off-chain thesis JSON the frontend reads); critical failure mode #5; CON-ai-interaction (timeline must read as a story, not a log). Fully code-checkable (length, JSON-validity, banned-token scan).

### Known Failure Modes in This Domain

<!-- How failure manifests specifically in AI copy-trading narration — not generic hallucination. -->
1. **On-chain contradiction.** The narration diverges from the settled swap (wrong direction/pair/token), so a judge clicking "Verify" finds the card's words and the chain disagree — the single most damaging failure, because it directly falsifies the verifiability claim the product is built on.
2. **Financial-promotion hype.** "Guaranteed," "10x," "to the moon," projected returns as certainty — the crypto-marketing failure mode FINRA/MiCA-style "fair, balanced, not misleading" norms exist to prevent, and the exact thing a compliance-aware panel (Hashed/Caladan) is primed to flag.
3. **Fabricated track record.** The thesis invents PnL, a win-rate, or a performance figure not grounded in the signal — corrosive because the whole pitch is that performance is computed *only* from on-chain history (REQ-06), so any fabricated number undercuts the premise even though it never touches the chain.
4. **Advice-liability framing.** Slips from "here is what this agent did" into "here is what *you* should do," reading as a personalized investment recommendation/solicitation — the copy-trading regulatory-optics trap the non-custodial framing is meant to sidestep.
5. **Persona drift.** Voice silently stops being the one locked momentum-trader across signals, breaking the agents-as-characters story the UI/UX prize rewards.

### Regulatory / Compliance Context

**Not hard legal compliance — institutional-panel optics on a Sepolia testnet demo.** This is a hackathon entry trading mock ERC-20s on Mantle Sepolia (D-41), so no live financial-promotion regime *binds* the thesis. But the panel is explicitly compliance-aware (Hashed, Caladan), and `Sequa Project.md` §10 flags copy-trading regulatory optics as a named risk a judge "may probe." The applicable *norms to mirror as optics* — not invoked as binding law — are well-established practitioner standards:
- **FINRA Rule 2210** ("fair, balanced, and not misleading"; promotional claims must balance benefit with risk; past performance must carry a "does not guarantee future results" qualifier and may not be presented as a promise). This is the standard the financial-promotion-safety dimension above operationalizes.
- **MiCA truth-in-marketing for crypto** (accurate, clear, non-misleading promotional communications; almost any content capable of influencing an investment decision is in scope; return-guarantee language disallowed). Relevant because shared cards are public crypto-promotional content (REQ-11/REQ-14).
The project's own engineered mitigation is the rubric anchor: **frame as "follow a verifiable strategy," not "we manage your money"; descriptive-not-prescriptive; no performance guarantees; non-custodial by design** (`Sequa Project.md` §10; REQ-03). Characterize accurately: capture these as a real evaluation constraint and a domain failure mode, do **not** overstate them as legal obligations governing a testnet demo. No invented regulations.

## 2. Framework Decision

**Selected Framework:** Official Anthropic TypeScript SDK (`@anthropic-ai/sdk`) used as a **direct client — no agent framework**.

**Version:** Pin the latest stable `0.x` at install time (`npm install @anthropic-ai/sdk` then lock the exact version in `package.json` / `package-lock.json`). `gsd-ai-researcher` confirms the concrete current version in Section 3.

**Rationale:**
The trade decision is a deterministic MA-crossover rule kept off the mirror hot path (D-01); Claude is called exactly once per signal to produce a 1–2 sentence narration (Content Generation, D-08/D-09). There is no agent loop, tool use, multi-step planning, RAG, or state graph — so `ai-frameworks.md` anti-pattern #1 governs: *"a direct SDK call is less code, faster, and easier to debug."* Provider is locked to Anthropic (D-01, CON-ai-interaction) and language to TypeScript/Node 20, so the **first-party SDK** is the smallest reliable surface: one typed dependency with built-in retries, timeouts, request/response types, and clean system+user prompt + model pinning — exactly what the consistent momentum persona (D-08) and the <3-min reproducible local boot (D-39) need on an 8-day hackathon runway. **Confirmed by the user at the orchestrator level** over the Vercel AI SDK alternative.

**Alternatives Considered:**

| Framework | Ruled Out Because |
|-----------|------------------|
| Vercel AI SDK (`ai` + `@ai-sdk/anthropic`) | Its core advantage is provider-swap abstraction, which is dead weight when the provider is locked to Claude (D-01) and there is no shared Next/Vercel call in Phase 1 scope. Viable later if the Phase 4 frontend wants to share this exact call; user chose the direct SDK for Phase 1. |
| LangChain / LangGraph | Agent/chain/graph frameworks for multi-step stateful flows. Over-scoped for a single completion; anti-pattern #1 ("don't use LangChain for simple single calls") and #5 ("don't default to LangChain without evaluating alternatives"). Abstraction overhead works against the <3-min boot and debuggability. |
| Claude Agent SDK | Built for code-aware autonomous agents with MCP / filesystem / shell / 18 lifecycle hooks. Radically over-scoped for a 1–2 sentence narration call, and its newer/evolving API conflicts with the reproducibility requirement (D-39). |
| Raw `fetch` to the Anthropic REST API | Saves one dependency but reimplements retries, timeouts, typing, and error handling by hand under deadline — false economy versus the maintained first-party SDK. |

**Vendor Lock-In Accepted:** **Yes — Anthropic, consciously.** Claude is locked by design (D-01, CON-ai-interaction: "agents as characters"); the product's entire identity is being *Claude-driven*. The lock-in risk is contained because the thesis is non-critical off-chain commentary (D-09): if Anthropic is unavailable, the agent still records signals and swaps on-chain — only the narration degrades — so the verifiable core never depends on the provider. The direct SDK (vs. an abstraction layer) makes the lock-in explicit rather than hidden, which is the correct trade for a single-provider product.

### Entry Point Pattern
```typescript
// narrateSignal.ts — ONE single-shot Claude call per deterministic signal (D-08).
// This is NOT an agent: no loop, no tools, no history. Input = the current signal only.
import Anthropic from '@anthropic-ai/sdk';

// Module-level singleton: one client, reused for all ~20 calls/day. Do not construct per call.
const client = new Anthropic({
  // apiKey defaults to process.env.ANTHROPIC_API_KEY — shown for clarity, can be omitted.
  apiKey: process.env.ANTHROPIC_API_KEY,
  maxRetries: 2,        // SDK auto-retries 429/408/409/>=500/connection errs with backoff (default 2).
  timeout: 8_000,       // 8s hard cap PER CALL — the thesis is off the hot path, never let it hang (D-01/D-09).
});

// Static persona — the single momentum-trader voice (D-08/D-12). Defined ONCE, reused every call.
const PERSONA_SYSTEM_PROMPT = `You are MOMENTUM, a confident momentum trader. You are patient until a
trend confirms, then you enter fast and exit faster on reversal. You narrate a single trade decision
that has ALREADY been made by a deterministic moving-average crossover rule — you do not decide, you
explain. Write exactly 1-2 sentences in first person. Never invent prices, PnL, percentages, or
guarantees. Never give financial advice. State only what the signal tells you: the pair, the
direction, and that a short MA crossed a long MA.`.trim();

// The signal object emitted by the deterministic strategy core (decimals per D-18).
interface Signal {
  agentId: string;     // ERC-8004 agentId (D-27) — needed to key the thesis JSON (D-09); used in §4 narrateAndStore
  pair: string;        // e.g. "WMNT/USDC"
  direction: 'BUY' | 'SELL';
  shortMa: number;     // 5-tick MA (D-03)
  longMa: number;      // 20-tick MA (D-03)
  sizeUsdc?: number;   // fixed-fraction sizing on BUY (D-07); omitted on flat-sell
}

export async function narrateSignal(s: Signal): Promise<string> {
  // User turn carries ONLY the current signal as compact facts — no chat history (stateless).
  const userContent =
    `Signal: ${s.direction} ${s.pair}. ` +
    `Short MA ${s.shortMa.toFixed(4)} crossed long MA ${s.longMa.toFixed(4)} ` +
    `(${s.direction === 'BUY' ? 'above' : 'below'}).` +
    (s.sizeUsdc ? ` Sizing ~${s.sizeUsdc} USDC.` : '');

  const msg = await client.messages.create({
    model: 'claude-haiku-4-5',   // cheap+fast for a 1-2 sentence narration (rationale in Section 4).
    max_tokens: 120,             // hard ceiling — 1-2 sentences fit well under this (D-08 length budget).
    temperature: 0.7,            // a little voice/variety without drifting off-persona.
    system: PERSONA_SYSTEM_PROMPT,
    messages: [{ role: 'user', content: userContent }],
  });

  // response.content is an array of content blocks; for a plain text reply read the first text block.
  const block = msg.content[0];
  if (block?.type !== 'text') throw new Error('Unexpected non-text content block from Claude');
  return block.text.trim();
}
```

### Key Abstractions
| Concept | What It Is | When You Use It |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| `new Anthropic({...})` client | The typed Messages API client; holds `apiKey`, `maxRetries`, `timeout`. Construct once, reuse. | Module-level singleton shared by every narration call. |
| `client.messages.create(params)` | Single request/response Messages call. Returns `Anthropic.Message`. | The entire AI surface here — one call per signal, awaited. |
| `system` vs `messages` | `system` = the static persona (string, or array of text blocks for caching); `messages` = the per-signal user turn. | Persona in `system` (D-08), signal facts in the user turn. Never mix them. |
| `content[]` blocks + `block.type` | The response is an array of typed blocks; text lives in blocks where `type === 'text'`. | Read `content[0].text` after a `type === 'text'` guard — required for TS type-narrowing. |
| `Anthropic.APIError` / `APIConnectionTimeoutError` | Typed error classes (`.status`, `.name`, `.headers`). | `catch` to trigger graceful degradation (placeholder + retry-later), never to block the swap. |
| `cache_control: { type: 'ephemeral' }` | Marks a `system` text block as a prompt-cache breakpoint (5-min TTL default). | Only helps if the persona exceeds the model's min cacheable length — see Section 4b.5 caveat. |

### Common Pitfalls
1. **Reading `response.content` as a string.** `content` is an **array of content blocks**, not text. `response.content[0].text` only type-checks after a `block.type === 'text'` guard; skipping the guard fails the TS build and can throw at runtime on tool/thinking blocks. (Critical failure mode #5: empty/garbled thesis corrupts the off-chain JSON.)
2. **Letting the call sit on the hot path / no timeout.** The SDK default request timeout is **10 minutes**, and it auto-retries failures — a rate-limited Claude could stall ~30 min before giving up. With `recordSignal → swap` as the real path (D-01/D-09), you MUST set a small per-call `timeout` (e.g. 8s) and run narration **after** the swap returns, fire-and-store, never `await`-blocking the trade. (Critical failure mode #2.)
3. **Persona drift from prompt-in-the-user-turn.** Putting persona instructions in the `messages` user turn (instead of `system`) dilutes the voice and invites the model to "decide" the trade rather than narrate it. Keep the persona 100% in `system`; keep the user turn to bare signal facts. (Critical failure mode #4; also guards #1 — wrong direction.)
4. **Unbounded `max_tokens`.** Leaving `max_tokens` large lets the model write a paragraph, blowing the 1-2 sentence budget and inflating cost/latency. Pin a small `max_tokens` (≈120). (Critical failure mode #5.)
5. **Constructing a new client per call.** `new Anthropic()` per narration re-reads env and discards keep-alive sockets. Build one module-level singleton. (Latency + needless overhead on a 30s poll loop.)
6. **Assuming the persona system prompt is prompt-cached.** Caching is silently skipped below the model's minimum cacheable length — **4,096 tokens for Haiku 4.5**. A short persona prompt is well under that and will NOT cache; do not budget a cache discount you won't get (Section 4b.5).
7. **Pinning a wrong/dated model ID.** Use the dateless pinned alias `claude-haiku-4-5` (or the snapshot `claude-haiku-4-5-20251001`). A typo'd or deprecated ID throws `NotFoundError`/`BadRequestError` at the first signal — verify against the models doc before boot.

## 4. Implementation Guidance

**Model Configuration:**

| Parameter | Value | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| `model` | **`claude-haiku-4-5`** (snapshot `claude-haiku-4-5-20251001`) | **Recommended.** The task is a 1-2 sentence persona narration of an *already-decided* signal — near-frontier intelligence is unnecessary, but **lowest latency** (fastest tier) and **lowest cost** ($1/$5 per MTok) directly serve the off-hot-path, ~20-calls/day, <3-min-boot constraints (D-01/D-10/D-39). Haiku 4.5 easily holds a consistent voice and copies pair/direction faithfully at this scale. |
| `temperature` | `0.7` | Enough variety to sound human across signals without drifting off-persona or fabricating facts. Drop to `0.3` if you observe voice/format wobble in eval (Section 5). |
| `max_tokens` | `120` | Hard ceiling for 1-2 sentences (D-08). Never leave unbounded — caps cost, latency, and format blowout (failure mode #5). |
| `system` | static `PERSONA_SYSTEM_PROMPT` | Encodes the single momentum-trader voice + the "narrate, don't decide / don't fabricate" guardrails (D-08, failure modes #3/#4). |
| client `timeout` | `8_000` ms | Per-call hard cap so a slow/rate-limited Claude can never delay the swap (failure mode #2). |
| client `maxRetries` | `2` | SDK default; fine because retries happen *off* the hot path. After retries exhaust → fallback text. |

> **Model alternative considered:** `claude-sonnet-4-6` ($3/$15) buys marginally richer prose but 3x input / 3x output cost and higher latency for zero correctness gain on a copy-the-signal narration. **Reserve Sonnet 4.6 only if** eval (Section 5) shows Haiku materially failing persona fidelity or direction accuracy. `claude-opus-4-8` is categorically over-scoped (reasoning model for agentic coding) and is ruled out. **Pin Haiku 4.5.**

**Core Pattern — single-shot narration, OFF the on-chain hot path (graceful degradation is paramount):**
```typescript
// index.ts (poll loop, abbreviated) — the ORDER here is the whole safety story.
// 1) deterministic decision  2) on-chain record+swap (the REAL path)  3) fire-and-store narration.
import { narrateSignalSafe } from './narration/narrateSignal';
import { writeThesis } from './store/thesisStore';

async function handleSignal(signal: Signal) {
  // --- HOT PATH: must complete regardless of Claude ---
  const signalId = await recordSignalThenSwap(signal);   // SourceRegistry.recordSignal → exactInputSingle (D-25)
  if (signalId === null) return;                          // swap reverted → invalidateSignal already emitted (D-30)

  // --- OFF HOT PATH: narration is fire-and-store. Never await it before returning from the trade. ---
  // No `await` blocking the loop: schedule it so the next 30s tick is never delayed by Claude.
  void narrateAndStore(signal, signalId);                // intentionally un-awaited (see note in 4b.2)
}

async function narrateAndStore(signal: Signal, signalId: string) {
  const thesis = await narrateSignalSafe(signal, signalId);  // never throws — returns fallback on failure
  await writeThesis(signal.agentId, signalId, thesis);       // theses/<agentId>/<signalId>.json (D-09/D-37)
}
```
```typescript
// narration/narrateSignal.ts — narrateSignalSafe wraps the raw call with validate + retry + fallback.
import { thesisSchema } from './thesisSchema';
import { fallbackThesis } from './fallback';

export async function narrateSignalSafe(signal: Signal, signalId: string): Promise<string> {
  try {
    const raw = await narrateSignal(signal);          // the entry-point call (Section 3), timeout-bounded
    const parsed = thesisSchema.safeParse({ thesis: raw });
    if (parsed.success) return parsed.data.thesis;
    // ONE structured-output retry with a tightening nudge (see 4b.1) before giving up.
    const retry = await narrateSignal(signal);
    const reparsed = thesisSchema.safeParse({ thesis: retry });
    if (reparsed.success) return reparsed.data.thesis;
    console.warn({ signalId, reason: 'thesis_validation_failed' });  // structured stdout (D-38)
    return fallbackThesis(signal);                    // deterministic placeholder, still on-persona
  } catch (err) {
    // APIError / timeout / connection: log + degrade. The trade already settled — never rethrow up the loop.
    console.warn({ signalId, reason: 'thesis_call_failed', err: String(err) });
    return fallbackThesis(signal);
  }
}
```

**Tool Use:** **None.** This is deliberately a plain `messages.create` text completion — no Claude tool use, no function calling, no MCP. The deterministic strategy core (not the LLM) decides every trade (D-01), so giving Claude tools would reintroduce exactly the nondeterminism the architecture removes. (If structured output is later preferred over free-text-plus-Zod, a single-tool `tool_choice` JSON-mode could be used, but it is unnecessary for a one-field short string — see 4b.1.)

**State Management:** **Stateless per call — no conversation memory, no RAG, no vector store.** Each narration receives only the current signal; there is no chat history threaded across calls. The only persisted artifact is the **output**: the thesis written to off-chain JSON keyed by `(agentId, signalId)` (D-09/D-37), which the agent never reads back into a prompt. The strategy core holds its own (non-AI) rolling MA / position / cooldown state; that is independent of and invisible to Claude.

**Context Window Strategy:** **Non-issue by design.** Input per call is a static persona (~hundreds of tokens) plus one short signal sentence — a few hundred tokens total against Haiku 4.5's 200k window. There is nothing to truncate, summarise, rerank, or compact. The only context discipline that matters is the *output* ceiling (`max_tokens: 120`) and never accumulating history. Explicitly: do **not** add prior theses or market history to the prompt — it would add cost, invite drift, and buy nothing.

## 4b. AI Systems Best Practices

> Written by `gsd-ai-researcher`. Cross-cutting patterns every developer building AI systems needs — independent of framework choice. **Translated to TypeScript** (Zod replaces Pydantic).

### Structured Outputs with Zod

The output is a single short string, so the schema is intentionally tiny — its real job is enforcing the **1-2 sentence / length budget** and rejecting empty or JSON-breaking output (failure mode #5) before it reaches the off-chain thesis file the frontend consumes.

```typescript
// narration/thesisSchema.ts
import { z } from 'zod';

export const thesisSchema = z.object({
  thesis: z
    .string()
    .trim()
    .min(15, 'thesis too short / empty')           // guards the empty-output failure mode
    .max(280, 'thesis exceeds 1-2 sentence budget') // ~tweet-length ceiling for 1-2 sentences (D-08)
    .refine((t) => !/[\{\}`]/.test(t), 'thesis contains JSON/markdown-breaking characters'),
});

export type Thesis = z.infer<typeof thesisSchema>;
```

**How it integrates:** because the call returns plain text (no native JSON mode needed for one field), we validate *after* the call with `thesisSchema.safeParse({ thesis: rawText })` rather than a framework hook like LangChain's `.with_structured_output()`. (The direct-SDK choice in Section 2 means there is no framework wrapper — `safeParse` is the equivalent of `instructor`/Pydantic-validate for the Anthropic TS SDK.) If a richer object were ever needed, the SDK's `betaZodTool` + `tool_choice` could force JSON conforming to the Zod schema, but that is over-engineering for one string.

**Retry logic:** **one** retry on validation failure (the `narrateSignalSafe` wrapper in Section 4). Rationale: this is off the hot path and capped at ~20 calls/day, so a single retry is the right cost/latency trade — more retries waste budget on a non-critical artifact. On the retry, optionally append a one-line nudge to the user turn (`"Keep it to 1-2 short sentences, plain text only."`). **Log** `{ signalId, reason }` to structured stdout (D-38) on every failed parse. **Surface** = fall back to deterministic placeholder text (`fallbackThesis`) and move on; never surface as an error that blocks the loop, and never retry inline on the hot path.

### Async-First Design

The Anthropic TS SDK is **promise-based**: `client.messages.create(...)` returns a `Promise<Anthropic.Message>` you `await`. In Node 20 there is no event-loop-vs-`asyncio.run()` footgun (that is a Python concern) — the equivalent mistake here is **`await`-ing the narration on the hot path**, which would let a slow Claude delay or reorder `recordSignal → swap`. The correct pattern (Section 4) is: `await` the on-chain trade, then **fire-and-store** the narration with `void narrateAndStore(...)` so the 30s poll loop is never blocked by the LLM, with a per-call `timeout` (8s) and the SDK's built-in backoff handling transient 429/5xx.

**Stream vs. await:** **await, do not stream.** Streaming (`client.messages.stream(...)`) exists for perceived-latency UX on long generations surfaced live to a user. Here the output is ~1-2 sentences written to a JSON file and read later by the Phase 4 card — there is no live reader, and we need the **complete** text to run Zod validation before persisting. A single awaited `messages.create` is simpler, cheaper to reason about, and the right call.

> One caveat on the un-awaited `void narrateAndStore(...)`: attach a `.catch()` (or keep all throwing inside `narrateSignalSafe`, which we do) so a rejected promise can never become an unhandledRejection that crashes the long-running process. The wrapper returns a fallback instead of throwing, satisfying this.

### Prompt Engineering Discipline

- **System vs. user separation (strict):** the entire persona + guardrails live in `system` (`PERSONA_SYSTEM_PROMPT`); the user turn contains **only** the current signal's facts (pair, direction, MA values, optional sizing). This is what keeps the voice consistent across signals (D-08) and stops the model from "deciding" instead of narrating.
- **Few-shot, inline (not retrieved):** include **1-2 fixed example pairs** in the system prompt to lock tone and the narrate-don't-fabricate behavior. Inline because the examples are static and tiny — dynamic retrieval (RAG) would be pure overhead here. Example to embed in `persona.ts`:
  ```
  Example — input: "BUY WMNT/USDC, short MA crossed above long MA."
  Example — output: "The short average just punched above the long on WMNT/USDC, so I'm stepping in with the trend — this is the confirmation I was waiting for."

  Example — input: "SELL mETH/USDC, short MA crossed below long MA."
  Example — output: "Momentum rolled over on mETH/USDC as the short average dropped under the long, so I'm out fast before the reversal deepens."
  ```
- **Token budget:** set `max_tokens` **explicitly** (120) — never unbounded in production (failure mode #5). Keep the user turn terse; do not pad with market history.

### Context Window Management

This system type (single-shot content generation, no history) has effectively **no context-window problem** — see Section 4's Context Window Strategy. There is no RAG reranking/truncation, no conversation summarisation, and no agent compaction because there is no retrieval, no multi-turn conversation, and no agent loop. The only rules: (1) keep the persona prompt compact and static, (2) never accumulate prior theses or price history into the prompt, (3) bound the output with `max_tokens`. If the persona prompt ever grows large enough to matter, the right lever is prompt caching (below), not truncation.

### Cost and Latency Budget

**Per-call estimate (Haiku 4.5 @ $1 input / $5 output per MTok):**
- Input ≈ persona system prompt + few-shot + signal sentence ≈ ~500-800 tokens → ~$0.0005-0.0008 per call.
- Output ≈ ≤120 tokens (1-2 sentences) → ~$0.0006 per call.
- **≈ $0.001-0.0015 per signal.**

**Daily / monthly:** at the ~20 signals/day soft cap (D-10): **≈ $0.02-0.03/day → well under $1/month.** Even on Sonnet 4.6 (3x) this is ~$0.06-0.09/day. **Cost is a non-constraint at this volume** — optimise for correctness and graceful degradation, not pennies. (One free retry per call, as specced, changes nothing material.)

**Caching — honest caveat:** prompt caching the static persona is the textbook win, **but** the minimum cacheable prompt length is **4,096 tokens for Haiku 4.5**. A compact 1-2-sentence-narration persona is well under that, so `cache_control` would be **silently skipped** and buy nothing. Additionally, the default cache TTL is **5 minutes** while the per-pair cooldown is ~3-5 min and the daily volume is ~20 calls — calls are spaced far enough apart that the cache would routinely expire between them anyway. **Recommendation: do not rely on prompt caching at this scale.** It only becomes worthwhile if the persona/few-shot block is deliberately expanded past the Haiku threshold (or you switch to Sonnet 4.6, min 1,024 tokens) *and* call frequency rises — neither is true in Phase 1. Exact-match caching at the application layer is also pointless here because every signal's input differs.

## 5. Evaluation Strategy

> The eval target is the **off-chain 1–2 sentence thesis** (D-09) — *not* the trade decision. The trade is a deterministic MA-crossover rule with its own coverage: the replay harness over canned price series (`test/strategy.replay.test.ts`, CONTEXT.md) plus the reconciler CLI (`scripts/reconcile.ts`, D-40) that proves 100% of non-invalidated signals map to a settled swap. Those are the acceptance gate for the trade and are out of LLM-eval scope. This section evaluates only the narration.
>
> **Architecture of the eval, three tiers (cheapest first):**
> 1. **Code-based checks** (deterministic, run pre-publish in the runtime AND in CI) cover the two objectively-checkable dimensions — signal fidelity (#1) and format/containment (#5) — against the signal payload and the existing Section 4b Zod validator. These are the first line and the load-bearing online gate (Section 6).
> 2. **Promptfoo prompt-regression** (language-agnostic CLI, in CI) runs the real `claude-haiku-4-5` call over the canned signal dataset so prompt/model edits can't silently regress fidelity, format, or banned-phrase safety. Dovetails with the project's existing "deterministic replay harness over canned price series" discipline.
> 3. **LLM-as-judge** (a second, offline/batch Claude call — never on the hot path) scores the three subjective dimensions: financial-promotion-safety nuance (#2), advice-framing (#3), and persona coherence (#4). Per `ai-evals.md`, the judge must be **calibrated against the founder's labels (target ≥0.7 correlation)** before its scores are trusted; until then it is advisory only and humans rule.

### Dimensions

| # | Dimension | Rubric (PASS / FAIL in domain language) | Measurement Approach | Priority |
|---|-----------|------------------------------------------|----------------------|----------|
| 1 | **Signal fidelity** (anchor) | **PASS:** thesis names the correct pair and the correct direction (BUY⇄"stepping in / long / entering", SELL⇄"out / exiting / flat"), and any rationale is consistent with the trigger (a *cross-above* for BUY, *cross-below* for SELL). **FAIL:** opposite direction, wrong pair/token, or rationale contradicting the deterministic trigger. | **Code** — assert the thesis against the `(tokenIn, tokenOut, amountIn, minAmountOut, fee)` signal payload: pair tokens must appear (or their canonical symbols), and a direction-lexicon match must agree with `direction`. Deterministic; no model. LLM-judge is only a secondary backstop for subtle rationale contradictions. | **Critical** |
| 2 | **Financial-promotion safety** | **PASS:** descriptive, present/past tense about *this* move; no profit/return promise, no win-rate or PnL number, no "guaranteed / 10x / to the moon / printing / can't lose", no projected price target stated as certainty. **FAIL:** promises or guarantees a return, projects a target as certainty, cites a fabricated track-record/win-rate figure, or urges the reader to buy/mirror for guaranteed gain. | **Code pre-filter + LLM Judge.** Banned-phrase/claim regex (guarantee-words, multiplier-hype, `%`/PnL-number detector) is the hard gate; the LLM judge backstops nuance the regex misses (implied certainty without a banned word). | **Critical** |
| 3 | **No advice-liability framing** | **PASS:** narrates the agent's *own* decision in first person; no recommendation, solicitation, or personalized instruction to the reader; framing is "follow a verifiable strategy," never "we manage your money / I'll grow your funds." **FAIL:** second-person imperative reading as personalized advice ("you should buy now", "put your USDC in WETH"), or language implying custody/responsibility for the reader's funds. | **Code pre-filter + LLM Judge.** Regex flags second-person imperatives ("you should", "you need to", "buy now", "put your"); LLM judge scores the softer custody/advice framing the regex can't catch. | **High** |
| 4 | **Persona coherence** | **PASS:** voice is the one locked momentum trader — confident, "patient until the trend confirms, then in fast, out faster on reversal" (D-08); tone consistent across signals so the card reads as one personality. **FAIL:** drifts to a different archetype (cautious value-investor, hype-shiller), breaks character, or reads as a generic chatbot. | **LLM Judge**, calibrated to the D-08/D-12 persona spec (founder is the persona owner). Cross-signal consistency assessed by judging a *sampled batch* together, not one thesis in isolation. | **High** |
| 5 | **Format & containment** | **PASS:** 1–2 sentences, non-empty, trimmed length 15–280 chars, valid JSON string value, no `{` `}` or backtick characters, no leaked raw signal tuple / system-prompt fragment. **FAIL:** >2 sentences, empty, JSON/markdown-breaking characters, or leaked config/prompt/signal JSON. | **Code** — **reuse the existing Section 4b `thesisSchema` Zod validator** (min 15 / max 280 / no `[\{\}\`]`) plus a sentence-count check (≤2) and a raw-tuple/leakage scan. Do not reinvent; this validator already runs in `narrateSignalSafe`. | **Medium** |

**Calibration note (per `ai-evals.md`, mandatory before trusting the judge):** for dimensions 2, 3, 4 the LLM judge must reach **≥0.7 correlation with the founder's / compliance-optics reviewer's labels** on the reference set before its scores gate anything. Until calibrated, the judge output is advisory and the human label wins. The judge is itself a `claude-haiku-4-5` (or Sonnet 4.6 if Haiku judging proves noisy) call with an explicit PASS/FAIL rubric per dimension and a required one-line justification — run offline/batch, never on the narration hot path.

### Reference Dataset

**Size:** **12–18 canned signals** (start small per `ai-evals.md` "10–20 high-quality, not 200 mediocre"). Each is a `Signal` fixture (`pair`, `direction`, `shortMa`, `longMa`, optional `sizeUsdc`) plus the human-labeled expected-PASS/FAIL per dimension.

Flag for the auditor: do **not** expect theses for the skip cases; their correct behavior is *silence*, verified by the strategy replay harness, not by this dataset.

**Labeling:**
- **Founder / operator** (persona owner, D-08/D-12) is the primary labeler: assigns PASS/FAIL for persona coherence (#4) and signs off financial-promotion tone (#2), and gives final tone sign-off before boot (D-36). This is the human gold standard `ai-evals.md` requires — a domain expert, not just an engineer.
- **Compliance-optics reviewer** (founder or peer standing in for the Hashed/Caladan panel) adversarially labels the promotion/advice baits (#2/#3).
- **DeFi practitioner** confirms the signal-fidelity (#1) expected labels against the payloads.
- **Calibrated LLM judge** then scores at scale and is validated against these human labels (≥0.7 correlation) before being trusted for the offline flywheel. Code checks (#1, #5) need no human labeling beyond confirming the expected payload — they are deterministic.

## 6. Guardrails

> The load-bearing guardrail is the **online pre-publish validation gate**. It runs the deterministic code checks *before* the thesis JSON is written/published, and on failure substitutes the deterministic `fallbackThesis` (Section 4) and flags. **Hard invariant:** this gate operates entirely on the off-hot-path narration branch (`narrateAndStore`) — it must **never** block, delay, or reorder `recordSignal` or the `exactInputSingle` swap, both of which have already completed before narration is even attempted (Section 4 ordering; critical failure mode #2). The gate gates *publishing words*, never *trading*.

### Online (Real-Time)

| Guardrail | Trigger (per thesis, pre-publish, off hot path) | Intervention |
|-----------|--------------------------------------------------|--------------|
| **Format & containment gate** | `thesisSchema.safeParse` fails: empty, <15 or >280 chars, contains `{` `}` / backtick, or sentence-count > 2, or detects a leaked raw signal tuple / prompt fragment. | **One retry** with a tightening nudge (Section 4b); if still failing → **substitute `fallbackThesis(signal)`** + `console.warn({ signalId, reason: 'thesis_validation_failed' })` (D-38). Never publish the bad string. |
| **Signal-fidelity gate** (anchor) | Direction/pair assertion vs. the `(tokenIn, tokenOut, …)` payload fails — thesis implies the wrong direction or names the wrong pair/token. | **Block publish → substitute `fallbackThesis(signal)`** (which is generated deterministically from the payload, so it is fidelity-correct by construction) + flag `reason: 'signal_fidelity_mismatch'`. This is the most damaging failure mode (#1); never let a contradicting thesis reach the card. |
| **Banned-phrase safety gate** | Promotion regex (guarantee/10x/to-the-moon/printing/`%`-or-PnL-number) **or** advice regex (second-person imperative / custody language) matches. | **Block publish → substitute `fallbackThesis(signal)`** + flag `reason: 'promotion_phrase'` / `'advice_phrase'`. Keeps return-promise / solicitation framing off the public card and the X-share surface (REQ-11/REQ-14). |

All three run synchronously *inside* `narrateAndStore`, before `writeThesis`. The `fallbackThesis` substitution means a guardrail trip degrades to a plain, on-persona, fidelity-correct placeholder — the card is never blank and never wrong, and the trade is wholly unaffected.

### Offline (Flywheel)

| Metric | Sampling Strategy | Action on Degradation |
|--------|-------------------|------------------------|
| **Persona-coherence score** (LLM judge vs. D-08 spec) | Daily batch over a sampled set of published theses, weighted toward any that tripped a retry or whose temperature output looked off; plus a periodic *cross-signal* batch (judge several together) to catch slow drift across the card. | If mean score drops below the calibrated PASS bar → review the persona prompt; consider dropping `temperature` 0.7→0.3 (Section 4) or expanding the few-shot examples. |
| **Promotion / advice judge score** (nuanced, beyond the regex) | Daily batch over all theses the regex *passed* (catch implied certainty / soft solicitation the hard filter missed); 100% sample is cheap at ~20/day. | Any judge-flagged miss the regex let through → tighten the banned-phrase set and/or the persona guardrail line; re-run `eval:ci`. Recurrent misses → escalate model to Sonnet 4.6 for judging or narration. |
| **Fallback-substitution rate** | Count of theses that fell back to `fallbackThesis` per day (from the D-38 structured `reason` logs), bucketed by reason. | A rising fallback rate is the early-warning signal-metric-divergence indicator (`ai-evals.md`): if validation/fidelity/phrase trips climb, investigate prompt or model regression before the demo. |
| **Judge ↔ human calibration drift** | Re-check judge-vs-founder correlation on a fresh handful of theses weekly. | If correlation drifts below 0.7, the judge is no longer trustworthy → re-calibrate before relying on its flywheel scores. |

## 7. Production Monitoring

**Tracing Tool:** **Lean path (recommended for Phase 1): the existing D-38 observability stack — structured stdout thesis-event logs + `/healthz` + external UptimeRobot ping.** The project already ships this; for a single-model, off-hot-path narration running **~20 calls/day** (D-10), standing up a full tracing platform is disproportionate to the runway. Concretely, emit one structured log line per narration — `{ signalId, agentId, pair, direction, model, durationMs, retried, fallbackUsed, validationReason?, judgeScores? }` — to stdout (the same channel as the existing `console.warn` degradation logs). That *is* the trace at this volume: each line is one span of the narration "request," greppable and shippable to the demo-day log view.

> **Named upgrade (not Phase 1):** **Arize Phoenix** is the GSD default and has TypeScript/JS support via OpenInference/OpenTelemetry, so the Anthropic call can be auto-instrumented into Phoenix traces if/when volume or multi-agent scope grows (Phase 2 mirror engine, multiple sources). For Phase 1 it would be redundant infra over the already-built D-38 stack on an 8-day clock. **Recommendation: ship the lean path now; reach for Phoenix only if narration volume rises materially or you want a visual trace UI for the demo.** Do not prescribe both.

**Key Metrics to Track (from the structured logs + flywheel judge):**
1. **Fallback-substitution rate** — % of signals whose thesis fell back to `fallbackThesis`, bucketed by reason (`validation` / `signal_fidelity_mismatch` / `promotion_phrase` / `advice_phrase` / `call_failed`). The single best health number — it folds fidelity, format, safety, and API-availability failures into one trend.
2. **Thesis-call latency (`durationMs`) and timeout/error rate** — guards the off-hot-path budget (8s timeout, failure mode #2); a rising timeout rate signals Anthropic degradation before it shows on the card.
3. **Retry rate** — % of calls that needed the one validation retry; a leading indicator of prompt/model drift before it becomes a fallback.
4. **Persona / promotion judge pass-rate** — from the offline flywheel batch (report-only until the judge is calibrated ≥0.7).
5. **`/healthz` freshness** — `lastSignalAt` / `lastTickAt` staleness (D-38): the silent-VPS-dead detector; not thesis-specific but the umbrella liveness signal that the agent is producing signals at all.

**Alert Thresholds:**
- **Page / Discord-Slack alert (existing UptimeRobot path, D-38):** `/healthz` stale — `lastTickAt` older than ~3× the 30s poll (≈ 2 min) or `lastSignalAt` unexpectedly flat over a session. This is the catch-the-VPS-before-demo-day alert.
- **Investigate (not page):** **fallback-substitution rate > ~20%** over a rolling window, or a **signal-fidelity mismatch ever firing** (any single `signal_fidelity_mismatch` is worth a look — the anchor failure mode #1 must be ~0%), or **timeout/error rate climbing** toward saturating the 8s budget. These are quality-degradation signals, not liveness pages — the trade path is unaffected so they are not wake-someone-up events, but they erode the demo's credibility surface.
- **Cost:** no alert needed — at ~$0.02–0.03/day (Section 4b) cost is a non-constraint; track it only as a sanity line.

**Smart Sampling Strategy (for human / judge review, per `ai-evals.md` weight-toward-concerning-signals):**
- **Always review:** any thesis that tripped a guardrail (logged `fallbackUsed: true` with a `reason`) — these are the system telling you where it struggled, and they are the highest-signal review set.
- **Always review:** any `signal_fidelity_mismatch` and any `promotion_phrase` / `advice_phrase` trip — the Critical/High dimensions, before they reach a judge's eyes or the X-share surface.
- **Weighted sample of clean passes:** a small daily sample of theses that passed all code checks, fed to the offline LLM judge for persona/promotion-nuance scoring (catches the soft failures the regex can't). 100% sampling is affordable at ~20/day, so "sampling" here is really "judge them all, prioritize the flagged ones for human eyes."
- **Pre-boot human gate (Verify phase):** before the agent boots for its multi-day track record (D-36), the founder reviews the full reference-dataset run and signs off tone (the calibration step that makes the judge trustworthy).
